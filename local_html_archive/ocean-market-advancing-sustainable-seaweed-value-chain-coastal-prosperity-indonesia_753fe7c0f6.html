From Ocean to Market: Advancing a Sustainable Seaweed Value Chain for Coastal Prosperity in Indonesia”

    Description
    Description

    Since 2014, UNIDO, with funding from the Swiss Government through the State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO), has been supporting the development of seaweed sector in Indonesia through consecutive projects: SMART Fish (2014-2019), Global Quality and Standards Programme - GQSP I (2019-2023), and GQSP II (2023-2026). Implemented in collaboration with the Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fishery (MMAF) and the National Standardization Agency (BSN), the programme aims to strengthen quality infrastructure, enhance SME’s capacity to comply with standards and improve market access both national and international, as well as to create a culture of quality among all stakeholders.
    The programme’s unwavering commitment to supporting seaweed value chains is fueled by the immense potential and diverse benefits that seaweed offers. Economically, seaweed enhances the livelihoods of coastal communities, creating jobs, and fostering sustainable practices. Socially, it empowers local populations by promoting community engagement and resilience. Environmentally, seaweed farming provides service to environment such as producing oxygen and absorb CO2, reduce ocean acidification, and reduce pressure on wild fish stocks in coastal waters.

    In the upstream phase, the programme is dedicated to ensuring that seaweed farming practices are not just productive but also sustainable. This initiative begins with the development of comprehensive Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for responsible and sustainable seaweed farming, aligned with international standards such as Good Aquaculture Practices. These SOPs are then disseminated to seaweed farmers and local extension services through capacity-building activities designed to empower and educate. To guarantee the effective adoption of these practices, the Local Technical Assistants – selected from farmer leaders/champions and/or extension workers - were assigned to actively monitor and report on SOP implementation while providing valuable feedback and support to farmers, fostering continuous improvement within the farming community. In 2022, 36 farmer leaders/champions and 57 extension workers received intensive Training of Trainers on sustainable seaweed farming SOPs. By 2026, the project will establish 9 SOPs, 2 guidelines and other training modules for sustainable seaweed sector development.

    On the downstream side, the programme assists the seaweed processors in expanding their market. This is achieved by improving product quality through intensive capacity-building initiatives and hands-on technical assistance in areas like Good Handling Practices (GHP), Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP), and Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP). As of 2024, among five seaweed processor communities─ 80% of which are women-led ─ four groups have obtained Home Industry Food Production Certificates with programme's support. As a result, they expanded their market reach and increased sales twelvefold. This comprehensive support not only improves the quality of seaweed products but also empowers local actors to thrive in competitive markets.
    While the programme continues to support the mainstream industry, it also improves the innovation capacity through production of high-value species and value-added products such as bio-stimulant that serve as eco-friendly alternatives to chemical fertilizers both for aquaculture and agricultural sectors. This dual focus on sustainability and innovation makes the programme a driving force for positive change in the seaweed sector, promoting economic growth, while protecting the environment.
