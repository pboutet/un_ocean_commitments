    Description
    Description

    The Ocean Cleanup and Protection Initiative, led by Recyclamer, is a proactive, collaborative effort aimed at eliminating marine pollution by addressing both macroplastics and microplastics in the world’s oceans. This initiative focuses on combining innovative technologies, community involvement, and scientific research to protect and restore marine ecosystems and ensure the long-term sustainability of our oceans.

    At the core of this voluntary commitment is MR Turbino, a cutting-edge device designed to collect plastic waste from the ocean floor efficiently. The technology incorporates a suction system that can collect large debris and fine particles, such as microplastics, without harming marine life or disturbing the ocean’s natural environment. The device is crafted from recycled PETG, making it an eco-responsible and sustainable solution for ocean cleanup.

    The initiative recognizes that marine pollution is one of the greatest threats to global biodiversity and the health of our planet. In addition to harming aquatic life, pollution impacts coastal economies, disrupts the fishing and tourism industries, and contributes to broader environmental degradation. With this commitment, Recyclamer and its partners aim to take action on both local and global levels by creating a scalable and sustainable solution to fight ocean pollution.

    Partnerships play a central role in the success of this initiative. The Ocean Cleanup and Protection Initiative is supported by a broad network of stakeholders who each bring their expertise

    Partners

    For the Ocean Commitment led by Association Recyclamer, here is a list of potential organizations and entities that could be involved, along with their entity types:

    -Association Recyclamer (NGO/Environmental Organization)
    Lead entity for the Ocean Commitment, dedicated to marine conservation and pollution reduction.
    -PADI (Industry Association)
    Partnering for collaboration with diving centers, involving the diving community in the clean-up efforts.
    -Universities/Research Institutions (Academic Institutions)
    Contributing to research and development of the autonomous system, as well as data collection and analysis related to underwater pollution.
    -Geneseas Global Partners (Industry)
    Technical partner for expertise in underwater robotics and technology integration.
    -Local Governments/Environmental Agencies (Government Agencies)
    Supporting large-scale clean-up efforts and working on regulations in coastal areas and marine-protected zones.
    -Marine Conservation NGOs (NGOs/Environmental Organizations)
    Assisting with on-the-ground clean-up initiatives and raising awareness about the impact of marine pollution.
    -Research Labs/Technological Institutes (Research Institutions/Tech Industry)
    Collaborating on improving Turbino’s technology, including sensor systems, data analysis, and the filtration process.
    -Tourism and Diving Operators (Private Sector)
    Supporting clean-up activities by integrating them into tourism and diving operations, while promoting environmental awareness.

    Other beneficiaries
    Other beneficiaries of the Ocean Commitment may include: Local Communities (Community Groups) Benefiting from cleaner coastal areas, improved marine environments, and increased eco-tourism opportunities, which contribute to local economies and well-being. Fishermen and Aquaculture Industry (Private Sector) Cleaner oceans lead to healthier marine life, supporting the fishing and aquaculture industries by ensuring more sustainable resources and reducing damage from marine debris. Tourists (Public/Consumers) Eco-conscious travelers and divers benefit from a cleaner, healthier ocean environment, enhancing their experience and fostering a deeper connection to marine conservation. Environmental Scientists and Researchers (Academic Institutions/Research Bodies) Gaining access to data collected from the clean-up efforts, which helps in advancing scientific research on marine pollution, ecosystem health, and sustainability. Wildlife and Marine Ecosystems (Environmental Impact) Directly benefiting from reduced pollution, as cleaner oceans improve the health of marine life, biodiversity, and overall ecosystem stability.
