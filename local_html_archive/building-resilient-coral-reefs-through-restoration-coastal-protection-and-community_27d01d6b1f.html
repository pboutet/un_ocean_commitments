    Description
    Description
    Coralive is a Swiss & Maldivian conservation NGO that combines hands-on field work and research efforts. Coralive commits to advancing coral reef restoration and ecosystem conservation through innovative techniques and community-driven initiatives. We consist of a large network of interdisciplinary professionals as well as private and public funders & partners. At the heart of Coralive is a genuine care and motivation for the ocean, coral reefs and everything connected to it. Our efforts focus on: Restoration: Employment various coral restoration techniques such as Mineral Accretion Technology (MAT), rope and table nurseries, ex-situ spawning, micro-fragmentation and coral relocation to revive damaged reefs and enhance biodiversity. Coastal Protection: Planting mangroves and seagrass beds and deploying shoreline barriers to stabilize ecosystems and protect communities from erosion and climate impacts. Data and Monitoring: Long-term tracking of restoration success and ecosystem health, supporting global research and informed conservation policies. Community Engagement: Training local reef guardians, creating educational programs, and fostering sustainable livelihoods linked to restored ecosystems. Through this commitment, Coralive addresses critical challenges, such as climate-induced coral and habitat degradation, while supporting SDG 14 and related goals.
    Partners

    Coralive (Lead NGO) Local community partners in Madagascar, Indonesia, and the Maldives International Foundations: Soneva Foundation, Fregate Island Foundation, Internatinoal Coral Reef Society Private Sector Partner: Soneva Resorts, V.sun, Amaala & Red Sea Global, Oaga Resorts Universities & Research Institutes: University of Bremen, McGill & National Research Council Canada, Coral Spawning Lab Government Entities: Asian Development Bank (ADB)
