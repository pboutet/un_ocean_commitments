Regional Cooperation in the Governance of Sargassum Inundations in the Caribbean and West Africa.

    Description
    Description

    The increasing frequency and severity of Sargassum inundations in the Wider Caribbean Region present significant challenges to biodiversity, tourism, fisheries, and coastal livelihoods. Recognizing the transboundary nature of this issue, the Cartagena Convention and its Protocols provide an essential framework for fostering regional cooperation, policy harmonization, and integrated management strategies. This voluntary commitment aims at strengthening regional responses to Sargassum influxes.
    Objectives
    1. Strengthen Regional Coordination among countries and institutions through the Cartagena Convention, SPAW Protocol, and LBS Protocol.
    2. Support countries in integrating Sargassum management into national policies, aligning with regional frameworks.
    3. Collaborate with regional and national stakeholders and policymakers by implementing public awareness and capacity building initiatives.
    4. Strengthen Regional and Transboundary Cooperation with the Abidjan Convention and international organizations to share best practices and research.
    Implementation Methodologies
    1. COP Decisions and Work Under SPAW & LBS
    o Implement decisions from the Cartagena Convention and the SPAW Protocol COP meetings through biennial work plans, reinforcing commitments to Sargassum management.
    o Support SPAW-RAC’s leadership in coordinating the SPAW Sargassum Working Group.
    o Facilitate structured dialogues between the SPAW and LBS Working Groups to address Sargassum’s ecological and pollution-related challenges.
    o Leverage existing subregional mechanisms (CARICOM, OECS, SICA, ACS) to align efforts.
    2. Regional Action Plan
    o Develop a Sargassum Working Group Action Plan under the Cartagena Convention, aligned with the Regional Strategy and Action Plan for Key Marine Habitats (2021-2030).
    o Provide technical support for the development of national policies and regulations.
    3. Survey and Research-Based Approaches in collaboration with regional and national partners
    o Use results from SPAW-RAC’s survey to identify key national needs and priorities.
    o Expand research on the ecological impacts of Sargassum management practices.
    4. Capacity Building and Awareness Raising
    o Develop Sargassum Knowledge Products in collaboration with regional partners
     Sargassum White Paper: Turning the Crisis into an Opportunity
     UNEP Foresight Brief on Sargassum
     Turning the Golden Tide: Engaging Women and Youth in Sargassum Influx Management
     Sargassum Influx Update for Entrepreneurs and SMEs
     Sargassum Influx Champions: Civil Society on the Frontline
     Sargassum Influx Update for Policy Makers
    o Develop training modules to be included in the Secretariat capacity building programs like the CaMPAM Training of Trainers in MPA management for government officials and community groups.
    5. Regional and Transboundary Cooperation
    o Strengthen collaboration with the Abidjan Convention to exchange best practices between the Caribbean and West Africa.
    o Partner with international institutions such as UNESCO-IOCARIBE and GEO Blue Planet for enhanced knowledge sharing.
    o Coordinate funding mechanisms with regional development banks and international donors.
    Governance and Follow-up Mechanisms
    • The SPAW and LBS Protocols will serve as a governance structure for regional Sargassum management.
    • Contracting Parties will report biennial progress through SPAW-COP and LBS-COP meetings.
    • Regular assessments of national and regional actions will be conducted through the Sargassum Working Group to ensure alignment with commitments.
    • Knowledge exchange between stakeholders will be facilitated through existing regional dialogues, technical publications, and capacity-building programs.

    End poverty in all its forms everywhere

    By 2030, eradicate extreme poverty for all people everywhere, currently measured as people living on less than $1.25 a day

    1.1.1

    Proportion of the population living below the international poverty line by sex, age, employment status and geographical location (urban/rural)

    1.2

    1.2.1

    Proportion of population living below the national poverty line, by sex and age

    1.2.2

    Proportion of men, women and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions according to national definitions

    1.3

    Implement nationally appropriate social protection systems and measures for all, including floors, and by 2030 achieve substantial coverage of the poor and the vulnerable

    Proportion of population covered by social protection floors/systems, by sex, distinguishing children, unemployed persons, older persons, persons with disabilities, pregnant women, newborns, work-injury victims and the poor and the vulnerable

    By 2030, ensure that all men and women, in particular the poor and the vulnerable, have equal rights to economic resources, as well as access to basic services, ownership and control over land and other forms of property, inheritance, natural resources, appropriate new technology and financial services, including microfinance

    1.4.1

    Proportion of population living in households with access to basic services

    1.4.2

    Proportion of total adult population with secure tenure rights to land, (a) with legally recognized documentation, and (b) who perceive their rights to land as secure, by sex and by type of tenure

    By 2030, build the resilience of the poor and those in vulnerable situations and reduce their exposure and vulnerability to climate-related extreme events and other economic, social and environmental shocks and disasters

    1.5.1

    Number of deaths, missing persons and directly affected persons attributed to disasters per 100,000 population

    1.5.2

    Direct economic loss attributed to disasters in relation to global gross domestic product (GDP)

    1.5.3

    Number of countries that adopt and implement national disaster risk reduction strategies in line with the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030

    1.5.4

    Proportion of local governments that adopt and implement local disaster risk reduction strategies in line with national disaster risk reduction strategies

    Ensure significant mobilization of resources from a variety of sources, including through enhanced development cooperation, in order to provide adequate and predictable means for developing countries, in particular least developed countries, to implement programmes and policies to end poverty in all its dimensions

    Total official development assistance grants that focus on poverty reduction as a share of the recipient country’s gross national income

    1.a.2

    Proportion of total government spending on essential services (education, health and social protection)

    Create sound policy frameworks at the national, regional and international levels, based on pro-poor and gender-sensitive development strategies, to support accelerated investment in poverty eradication actions
