Create a mechanism to compensate fishermen for potential losses of income related to the restricted access to the Azores Marine Protected Areas Network

Environment Fund (
Government

    Description
    Description

    Marine Protected Areas are recognised as the most effective tool in the global effort to reverse marine biodiversity loss and achieving sustainable ocean management. The Marine Protected Areas established in Portuguese maritime space are fundamental to to this goal and essential to achieve the protection and regeneration of marine ecosystems contributing to other international commitments, like Target 3 under the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework to achieve 30% of protected marine areas, until 2030.
    The Azores Marine Protected Areas was a Portuguese commitment presented in 2024, by the Regional Government of Azores, as the outcome of a process that used the best available scientific knowledge and promoted a bottom-up collaborative approach with various stakeholders, that co-designed it. Their establishment reflect a national commitment and are the result of a highly participatory process involving stakeholders at different levels. Its implementation is the ground-base for a truly sustainable blue economy but also creates the need to establish compensation mechanisms for the loss of economic activity, specially focused on fishermen communities. The Environmental Fund defines the amount that can be used for compensation for commercial fishing, in order to minimise the short-term impacts of the creation of these Marine Protected Areas, particularly by the restrictions on access to certain areas, with potential losses of income.
