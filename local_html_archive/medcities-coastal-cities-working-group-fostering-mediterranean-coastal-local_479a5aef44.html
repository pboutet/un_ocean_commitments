    Description
    Description

    The MedCities working group on coastal cities has the objective to reinforce and give visibility to the role of local governments on the achievement of global biodiversity and sustainability targets (GBF, SDG) with relation to coastal resilience and blue economy. To do so, MedCities promotes the exchange of knowledge between the city members of the network to support the design and implementation of local actions on coastal management and protection. The group currently brings together 14 member cities and focuses on interesting methodologies, tools, processes and concrete best practices, and join advocacy on the topics of interest.
    The topics identified that constitute the core content of the group’s work program include:
    • Port Authority-City Council relation at the governance level
    • City-based sources of marine pollution
    • Ecosystem-based coastal management, with a focus on three challenges: (1) coastal adaptation to climate change; (2) interactions and integration among coastal cities and coastal and marine ecosystems (and protected areas) including urban coastal ecosystems restoration and protection; and (3) citizen engagement and participatory sciences approaches
    • Sustainable blue economy development challenges and opportunities, including tourism.

    MedCities advocates the crucial role of local authorities on the implementation of global, regional and national policies and the need to bring this level into the policy development processes to consider their needs and knowledge and provide with the necessary means for their local action.

    Partners

    Introduction
    The Associació MedCités / MedCities is an association of local authorities, with its headquarters in Barcelona, dedicated to sustainable urban development in the Mediterranean. It comprises 79 municipalities and unions of municipalities from seventeen different states and runs projects in the fields of strategic urban planning, urban services, environment, nature and biodiversity and local economic and social development, as well as training activities, technical support and the capitalisation of best practices. The network was created in 1991, since which time it has carried out dozens of projects in Mediterranean cities.

    Proportion of total research budget allocated to research in the field of marine technology

    Provide access for small-scale artisanal fishers to marine resources and markets

    14.c.1

    Number of countries making progress in ratifying, accepting and implementing through legal, policy and institutional frameworks, ocean-related instruments that implement international law, as reflected in the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea, for the conservation and sustainable use of the oceans and their resources
