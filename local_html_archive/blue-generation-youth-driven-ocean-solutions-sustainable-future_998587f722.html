    Description
    Description

    Next Generation Global Connect (NGGC) commits to promoting ocean literacy, youth-led marine conservation, and sustainable blue economy practices through its global initiative titled "Youth for Ocean Action and Climate Resilience." This voluntary commitment aims to empower young people, especially from coastal and island nations, to actively engage in the protection of marine and coastal ecosystems, combat marine pollution, and advocate for climate-resilient livelihoods. The initiative aligns with SDG 14 (Life Below Water), and is rooted in inclusive, science-based action, capacity-building, and intercultural collaboration.

    The primary objectives of this commitment are to:
    (1) Raise awareness among youth on the impact of marine pollution, ocean acidification, and unsustainable fishing practices;
    (2) Promote sustainable marine and coastal ecosystem management with an emphasis on mangroves, coral reefs, and community-based conservation;
    (3) Enhance youth understanding of the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) and strengthen advocacy for its implementation;
    (4) Facilitate youth participation in scientific research, data-sharing, and ocean innovation through partnerships with academic institutions and marine technology hubs.

    Implementation will be driven by regional chapters in collaboration with local NGOs, universities, and UN-affiliated programs. Activities will include community clean-ups, marine biodiversity workshops, virtual awareness campaigns, and youth leadership summits focused on marine sustainability. Special emphasis will be placed on youth from vulnerable communities—including small island developing states (SIDS), coastal African nations, and indigenous populations—ensuring equitable access to training and resources.

    The initiative will be governed by NGGC’s Climate and Ocean Action Committee, in partnership with marine science advisors, youth ambassadors, and civil society stakeholders. A digital monitoring system will track outreach, training sessions, project outcomes, and policy engagement. NGGC will produce annual progress reports, shared with stakeholders and published online to ensure transparency.

    Through this commitment, NGGC seeks to contribute meaningfully to the Ocean Decade by building a network of empowered youth who can serve as catalysts for sustainable change, preserving ocean health for present and future generations.
